## 📌 THREAD: Iran Internal — Chaharshanbe Suri as Potential Breaking Point

**Status: POTENTIAL ESCALATION**

**~05:00 CET, Mon Mar 16 — BBC Persian** (London): Report on reinforced checkpoints in Tehran, internet blocked, mass government SMS warnings to citizens. Residents describe checking their clothes, avoiding bright colors, flattering guards at checkpoints. Starlink is being sold on the black market to bypass the blackout. [[link]](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clygdwqw5dvo)

**Reza Pahlavi** called on Iranians to widely celebrate Chaharshanbe Suri (tomorrow evening, Tuesday) as an act of national solidarity against the regime, and asked the US and Israel to **support the Iranian people** if Tehran tries to suppress public gatherings. He said he might "celebrate Nowruz in Tehran this year" — language suggesting he may be actively seeking a political role in Iran's future. (Euronews, Mar 16)

**MEE:** A growing share of the same Iranians who saw Pahlavi as an alternative are **losing faith in him**, believing he is unable to unite the fragmented opposition. [[link]](https://www.middleeasteye.net/news/reza-pahlavis-supporters-iran-are-turning-against-him)

**Delta:** Chaharshanbe Suri has historically served as cover for neighborhood clashes. With Israel using social media videos to locate and strike Basij militia checkpoints, tomorrow's festival is an uncontrollable variable — for neither the regime nor the opposition.

## 📌 THREAD: China — Summit or Hormuz, Choose

**Status: EVOLVING**

**~12:00 CET, Mon Mar 16 — SCMP/FT**: Trump explicitly tied his **first China visit of his second term** (planned for late March) to Beijing's cooperation in reopening the Strait of Hormuz. "It's only appropriate that people who are the beneficiaries of the strait will help to make sure that nothing bad happens there." [[link]](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3346792/china-under-pressure-trump-ties-high-stakes-summit-strait-hormuz-crisis)

China is the world's largest buyer of Gulf oil. China's Foreign Ministry **pushed back against the demand** while reiterating its non-interference position, but emphasized the "irreplaceable" value of the summit as a diplomatic event.

**Delta:** Beijing is trapped: yielding on Hormuz legitimizes Trump's coercive logic; refusing risks derailing the summit. The Trump-Xi visit is now a test of China's strategic consistency.
