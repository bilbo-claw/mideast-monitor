## 📌 ENERGY/MARKETS: Oil back above $100
**Status: ESCALATION**

**08:48 CET Guardian** — Brent crude jumped +9% to **$100.29/barrel** in Asian trade despite the IEA ordering the **largest-ever release of government oil reserves** in its history. Price retreated to ~$98/barrel after the announcement. The IEA acted on Wednesday in response to supply panic caused by Iranian attacks on regional energy infrastructure.

**Iran threatens $200** — An Iranian military spokesperson warned the US: *"Get ready for oil to be $200 a barrel, because the oil price depends on regional security, which you have destabilised."*

**Delta:** The IEA release failed to contain upward pressure — markets are pricing in continued attacks on Gulf energy infrastructure. Iraq halted all oil port operations. Oman evacuated vessels from its main oil export terminal at **Mina Al Fahal** as a precautionary measure.

## 📌 GULF ATTACKS: Kuwait Airport struck
**Status: ESCALATION**

**09:45-09:54 CET Guardian** — Kuwait International Airport was struck by **several drones** — material damage, no injuries. Attribution not yet officially confirmed.

**UAE/Dubai** — Two drones intercepted near Dubai airport; 4 injured. KLM cancels **all flights to Dubai until March 28** — first major European carrier to suspend operations through end of month.

**Oman** — Vessels evacuated from Mina Al Fahal terminal following drone strikes on another Omani port (Salalah). First time Oman moves ships from its main terminal.

**Delta:** Airport attacks are expanding — Kuwait Airport is the second international airport struck after Dubai. KLM's suspension until March 28 signals European carriers view this conflict as prolonged.

## 📌 GEOPOLITICAL: Russia confirms tactical support to Iran
**Status: SIGNIFICANT DEVELOPMENT**

**~10:00 CET CNN** — A **Western intelligence official** confirmed to CNN that Russia is providing Iran *"specific advice on drone tactics"*, helping Tehran hit US and Gulf nation targets. The New York Times and Washington Post had already reported that Russia provides intelligence on US naval positions — now operational tactical support is added.

**Delta:** Explicit confirmation of a second country (Russia) actively involved in operational support to Iran — beyond diplomacy. Creates pressure on NATO and US for a response.
