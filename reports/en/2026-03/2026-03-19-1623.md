## 📌 THREAD: Energy Impact / Gas & LNG

**Status: ESCALATION**

**~15:00 CET** QatarEnergy — CEO Saad al-Kaabi confirms to Reuters: Iranian attacks on Ras Laffan have **knocked out approximately 1/5 of Qatar's LNG export capacity**; repairs expected to take **3-5 years**. ([Reuters via NYT](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/19/world/middleeast/qatar-natural-gas-attacks-ras-laffan.html))

**Delta:** The structural extent of the damage was not previously clear. The 3-5 year estimate transforms the event from a "tactical interruption" into **permanent infrastructure damage**. Ras Laffan is the world's largest LNG terminal.

**~15:00 CET** WSJ/Kuwait — Kuwait Petroleum Corp. (KPC) **formally suspends operations** at the **Mina Abdullah** (346,000 b/d) and **Mina Al-Ahmadi** (454,000 b/d) refineries after drone attacks — total ~800,000 b/d offline. Kuwait's three refineries were already operating at ~50% capacity before the attacks. ([WSJ via Corriere](https://www.corriere.it/esteri/diretta-live/26_marzo_19/guerra-in-iran-le-ultime-notizie-in-diretta-trump-non-vuole-altri-attacchi-agli-impianti-energetici-iraniani-missili-iraniani-su.shtml))

**Delta:** Previously only drone/fire was reported; now total shutdown confirmed — **4th GCC country with energy infrastructure offline**.
